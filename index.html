01 — About

Crafting worlds
through sound & code

I'm an interdisciplinary creator pursuing an MFA in Music Technology / Creative Computing at the California Institute of the Arts, following a BFA in Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts from UC San Diego.

My work explores the intersection of sound, code, and interactive media.I design systems and soundscapes that respond to the world around them — shaped through code, guided by physical processes, and grounded in the belief that sound is the most emotional dimension of any experience.

Alongside my creative practice, I bring over four years of professional experience in recording, mixing, post-production, and live event A/V, which has shaped my ability to work fluidly within fast-moving, collaborative environments.

Sound Tools

Max/MSP — Patching the Invisible

Max/MSP is my primary environment for generative audio systems, sensor-driven installations, and real-time DSP chains. Patches are native apps — but here's a look at the work.

⌥ Max/MSP Patch Preview Add a screenshot or screen recording
of your Max patch here

Generative Audio Engine

Building real-time DSP chains that respond to sensor input — accelerometers, proximity sensors, microphone analysis — to create soundscapes that feel physically connected to space.

Patches include granular synthesis engines, spectral processing chains, chaos-driven parameter modulation, and spatial audio routing for multi-speaker installations.

Tools: Max/MSP, gen~, mc. (multichannel), RNBO for hardware export, and OSC for communication with Arduino / TouchDesigner.

Why can't this run in the browser? Max/MSP patches are native desktop applications. To share them interactively, I'd export via RNBO or record a video walkthrough.
